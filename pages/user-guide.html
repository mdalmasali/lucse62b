Getting Started

No Login Required
1
Go to the Portal
Open your browser and visit lucse62b.xyz. You will land on the Home page. Some sections are public, but most features require you to be logged in.
2
First Time? Set Your Password
If you have never logged in before, click Login in the top navbar, then click "Set Password" on the login page. Enter your 16-digit Student ID and follow the steps to create a new password.
3
Log In
Click Login in the navbar. Enter your Student ID (16 digits) and your password, then click Login. Once logged in, the navbar will show all available pages.
4
Forgot Password?
On the login page, click "Forgot Password". You will need to verify your identity using your Student ID and date of birth, then you can reset your password.
Your login session is saved in the browser. You do not need to log in again every time you visit, unless you clear your browser data or log out manually.

Class Info

Login Required

The Info page has multiple sections accessible from the left sidebar. Click any item to load its content. Each section is described in detail below.

Class Routine

The main weekly timetable for CSE 62B — shows every class slot with day, time, room, course code, and teacher initials. Always reflects the latest routine maintained by the class rep.

  • Switch batch/section — By default it shows 62B, but you can switch to view any other batch or section from the dropdown at the top.
  • My Courses tab — A personalised view: courses you excluded (skipping) are hidden, and any retake/improve sections you enrolled in (from the Retake & Improve section) are added automatically, colour-coded differently so you can see your complete personal timetable in one view.
  • Exclude a course — If you are not attending a regular 62B class (e.g. because you already passed it or are taking it elsewhere), click the × button on that course in the My Courses tab to hide it. This also removes it from the clash detection in Retake & Improve.
  • Download — Save the current routine as an Image (PNG) or PDF using the buttons at the top right.
Teacher Class Routine

Look up the weekly schedule of any teacher at Leading University — not just 62B teachers, but university-wide.

  • Autocomplete search — Start typing a teacher's name or initials in the search box. A dropdown appears with matching suggestions — click one to load their routine instantly.
  • Full weekly view — See all the batches and sections that teacher is currently teaching, along with day, time, room, and course details.
  • Useful when you need to find a teacher's free period or confirm which batches they teach.
Course Offer

Shows all courses being offered to CSE 62B this semester in a clean card layout — course code, full title, credit hours, sections available, and prerequisites (if any).

  • A summary bar at the top shows the total number of courses and combined credit hours for the semester.
  • If a course has a prerequisite, it is shown so you know which course you must have passed first.
All Batch Course Offer

Same as Course Offer but covers every batch — not just 62B. This is especially useful when you need to find which batches are being offered a course you need to retake.

  • Batch chips — Click any batch number at the top to instantly switch and see that batch's course list.
  • Retake/Improve highlighting — Once your DOB is entered, courses that you need to retake (F grade) are highlighted in red and courses to improve (D/C/C+/B−) are highlighted in orange, so you can quickly spot them in any batch's list.
Bus Schedule

Shows the university shuttle bus departure times and route information. The table is maintained by the class rep and updated whenever the university changes the bus timings.

Semester Info

A key-value information panel for the current semester — includes the semester name, academic year, semester start/end dates, and any other important academic timeline information maintained by the class rep.

All Course Group Link

Lists all course group links (WhatsApp or Messenger) for this semester so you can join course groups without searching for them yourself.

  • Each card shows the course code, course name, and a clickable Join Group link.
  • A Copy Code button lets you copy the course code to clipboard with one click — useful for pasting into assignments or forms.
Teacher Contact List

A directory of all teachers handling courses for 62B this semester, with their contact details.

  • Each card shows the teacher's full name, initials/acronym, the courses they teach, and their phone number.
  • A WhatsApp icon next to the number lets you open a WhatsApp chat with that teacher directly from the portal.
bKash Payment / Fees

Shows university fee information needed when paying via bKash or at the bank. The table has three columns:

  • Head of Fees — The name/category of the fee (e.g. Tuition Fee, Lab Fee, Library Fee).
  • Fees Code — The numeric code you need to enter when making the payment at the bank or bKash agent.
  • Writing / Method — Instructions on how to write the payment slip or proceed with the payment method.
Mid Term Routine

The exam schedule for the Mid Term examination — shows each course with its exam date, time, room/hall, and any other instructions provided.

  • When any Mid Term exam is 7 days or less away, a countdown widget automatically appears on the Home page so you never miss the deadline.
  • The countdown shows days, hours, minutes, and seconds remaining until the next exam.
Final Term Routine

The exam schedule for the Final Term examination — same layout as the Mid Term Routine but for final exams.

  • The same 7-day countdown rule applies — a widget on the Home page appears when a Final exam is approaching.
  • Both Mid and Final Term countdowns can appear simultaneously if exams from both terms overlap in the same 7-day window.

Retake & Improve

This is the most advanced section of the Info page. It automatically detects which courses you need to Retake (failed with F grade) or Improve (low grade: D, C, C+, or B−), then shows you every section of that course currently in the university routine — so you can find one that fits your schedule.

What is Retake?

If you received an F grade in a course, you must retake it — attend a section from another batch, sit for the exams again, and pass the course. The old F grade is replaced by your new grade in your CGPA.

What is Improve?

If you received a D, C, C+, or B− in a course, you can voluntarily retake it to earn a better grade. Only the higher of the two grades counts toward your CGPA. Improve is optional — you choose whether it is worth the effort.

First-time setup — Enter your Date of Birth

The portal fetches your results from the LU result system. To do this it needs your Student ID (already stored from login) and your Date of Birth. The first time you open Retake & Improve, a prompt will ask for your DOB — enter it once and it is saved permanently. Your retake and improve courses are detected automatically after that.

Manual Add / Remove

If a course is not detected automatically (e.g. results are not yet published), you can manually add it. Use the Search bar to find a course by code or name, then click Add to Retake or Add to Improve. Manually added courses show a Manual badge and can be removed anytime. Your manual list is synced to the cloud so it stays consistent across devices.

Section Table — Free vs Clash

For each retake/improve course, the portal scans the full university routine and lists every section (batch + section + teacher + schedule). Each section is checked against the 62B regular class schedule:

  • Free — This section's time does not conflict with any 62B class. You can attend it.
  • Clash — This section overlaps with a 62B class you already have. Attending both is not possible.
Enroll & My List

Once you decide on a section, click the Enroll button next to it. This saves your chosen section to My List — a personal record of which batch and section you are attending for each retake/improve course. You can also see which classmates have enrolled in the same section (names appear below each section row). To change your section, click Enroll on a different row — it automatically replaces the old one. To remove a course from My List entirely, go to the My List tab and click Remove.

The tabs show counts: Retake (n) and Improve (n) reflect how many courses were detected from your results plus any you added manually. My List shows only courses you have actively enrolled in.
All information on this page is pulled live from Google Sheets and reflects the most up-to-date data maintained by the class representatives.

Games

Public · Login for standings

The Games page hosts two multiplayer games you can play with classmates in real time — no app download needed. Guests can play without logging in, but only logged-in students earn points on the Overall Standings.

Draw & Guess

One player draws a word on the canvas while the rest try to guess it. The faster you guess, the more points you earn. The drawer also earns points based on how quickly each player guesses — so a clear drawing rewards everyone.

  • Create or Join — Click Create Room to start a lobby and share the room code with friends. Others click Join and enter the code.
  • Host settings — The host can set number of rounds, drawing time per turn, max players, and whether the room is private.
  • Drawing tools — Draw, Erase, Fill (flood fill), color palette, custom color picker, three brush sizes, Undo (button or Ctrl+Z), and Clear.
  • Scoring — Guessers earn 50–150 pts based on how quickly they guess. Drawers earn the same per guesser — faster guesses = more for both.
  • No-repeat words — Words shown to you are not repeated for 7 days (stored on your device), so you won't see the same word every session.
  • Connection status — A green/yellow dot next to each player name shows whether they are connected. If someone disconnects, they are given a 7-second grace period before being marked as left — so a quick page refresh does not remove you from the game.
  • Rejoin mid-game — If you accidentally close the tab, reopen the game page and you will automatically rejoin the same room if it is still running.
  • Auto-end — If fewer than 2 active players remain, the game ends automatically.
Find the Imposter

Everyone receives the same secret word — except one person, the Imposter, who gets nothing. Players discuss the word without saying it directly, then vote on who they think has no word.

  • Roles — After the host starts the game, each player secretly sees either the word or a "You are the Imposter" screen.
  • Discussion phase — A timer counts down while everyone gives clues or descriptions without saying the word outright. The imposter tries to blend in.
  • Vote phase — Each player votes for who they think is the imposter. The player with the most votes is revealed.
  • Word packs — Multiple themed packs: Movies, Sports, Animals, Food, and Bangla. Words are randomized and don't repeat within 7 days on your device.
  • Scoring — Every player earns +5 pts for participating. Correct voters (who voted for the imposter) earn +10 pts. The same connection and rejoin rules as Draw & Guess apply.
Overall Standings

A live leaderboard on the right side of the Games page combining points from both Draw & Guess and Find the Imposter — only for logged-in students.

  • Rating — Ranked by a fair weighted rating, not just total points. It blends your average points per game, your wins, and how many games you've played — so playing more games alone won't carry you, winning actually counts, and a lucky one or two games won't park you at the top.
  • Columns — Rating (the weighted score), Wins, and Played.
  • Your own row is highlighted in green so you can find yourself quickly.
Guests can play both games freely. But to appear on the Overall Standings and have your wins tracked, you must be logged in.

World Cup 26 Match Center

Public · Login for predictions

The FIFA World Cup 2026 Match Center lets you follow the full tournament schedule and predict match scores before kick-off.

Schedule Tab

Shows all World Cup matches with date, time (Bangladesh time), group, venue, and result (once played). Matches are grouped by date. A live ticker in the top bar scrolls through today's matches automatically.

Predict Tab

Predict the score of upcoming matches before they start. Logged-in users can submit one prediction per match.

  • Upcoming matches show a score-entry form — type your predicted score and click Save.
  • Once a match goes live, the prediction card locks and shows a LIVE NOW badge. You can still see your saved prediction — it is not hidden.
  • After the match ends, the actual result is shown alongside your prediction.
Groups & Standings Tab

Shows the current group standings for all World Cup groups — points, wins, draws, losses, and goal difference. Updates after each match result is entered.

Predictions require login. The schedule and group standings are publicly visible without an account.
